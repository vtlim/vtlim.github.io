Science

University of California, Irvine

2015-2020

Awarded research fellowship by National Science Foundation (NSF)

I completed my PhD at UC Irvine (UCI) where I conducted research in the area of computer-aided drug design. I applied and developed computational tools to study biomolecular systems, such as protein-ligand complexes. My goal was to reveal insights from molecular simulations to further the development of new therapeutics. My research was co-advised by David Mobley and Doug Tobias.

You can visit my research bio page here (archived version), and dive into the details of my work in my dissertation. My dissertation defense presentation is accessible on YouTube. Tech Transfer

UCI Invention Transfer Group

2018-2020

Technology transfer is the process of transferring technology from origin to application. The technology transfer office at UC Irvine (Invention Transfer Group, ITG) helps faculty, physicians, and researchers process invention disclosures, patent innovations, and connect to industry partners. As a fellow at UCI ITG, I conducted technology assessments and prior art searches, wrote invention summaries and non-confidential disclosures, and initiated market research of UCI technologies.

Technologies I worked on
  • Medical devices
  • Polymer chemistry
  • Electrochemical membranes
  • ... and many more!

During my fellowship, I took and passed the USPTO patent bar (with a <50% pass rate in 2020). I am now a registered patent agent with the US Patent Office.
